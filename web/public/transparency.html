How fact.ai Works — Transparency Disclosure

fact.ai is a browser extension that uses artificial intelligence to analyse legislation and estimate its position on the political spectrum.

AI-generated content

All scores, confidence levels, and uncertainty ranges displayed by fact.ai are generated by an AI language model. No human editor reviews or modifies individual scores before they are shown to you.

Methodology

The AI model scores legislation on a continuous scale from −1.0 (furthest left) to +1.0 (furthest right), calibrated against peer-reviewed political-science datasets including the Chapel Hill Expert Survey (CHES) and established manifesto-research benchmarks. The model applies structured scoring rules designed to produce consistent, reproducible results.

Confidence & uncertainty

Each score is accompanied by a confidence percentage and an uncertainty range. A higher confidence percentage means the model found stronger signals in the text. The uncertainty range shows the plausible interval within which the true position may fall. Scores with low confidence or wide uncertainty should be interpreted with particular caution.

Limitations

AI-generated analysis has inherent limitations:

  • Scores reflect textual content only and may not capture legislative intent, political context, or subsequent amendments.
  • Calibration data is drawn primarily from UK and US political systems; coverage of other jurisdictions may be less precise.
  • The model may produce different results as it is updated or recalibrated.
