1. Introduction

LexLens ("we," "us," or "our") provides a browser extension that analyzes legislative text for ideological positioning using artificial intelligence. We are committed to handling any personal data responsibly and transparently.

This Privacy Policy explains what data we process, why we process it, how we handle it, and what rights you have. It applies to all users of the LexLens browser extension, regardless of location.

This policy is written to comply with:

  • The EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, Regulation 2016/679)
  • The UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and Data Protection Act 2018
  • The ePrivacy Directive (Directive 2002/58/EC, as amended)
  • The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
  • The Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA)
  • The Colorado Privacy Act (CPA)
  • The Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA)
  • The Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA)

3. What Data We Collect (Phase 1)

3.1 Data We Do NOT Collect

In Phase 1, LexLens does not:

  • Create or require user accounts
  • Store browsing history
  • Track which legislation you view over time
  • Set cookies or use persistent identifiers (see Section 3.3)
  • Collect your name, email address, or any identifying information you provide
  • Retain IP addresses beyond server log retention (see Sections 3.2 and 8)

3.2 Data We Process During Operation

When you use LexLens to analyze a piece of legislation, the extension sends a request to our backend API. This request contains:

DataDescriptionBasis
IP address Your device's IP address is transmitted as a natural result of network requests to our backend server. Our backend server logs may retain IP addresses for up to 30 days for security and debugging purposes (see Section 8). LexLens does not use your IP address for analytics, profiling, or any purpose other than routing. Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) — necessary to route the request
Legislative text The text or URL of the legislation you have chosen to analyze is sent to our API. This text is public legislative material and does not contain personal data. Legitimate interests — necessary to provide the service
Analysis output The ideology score and confidence rating generated by our AI model. This is not personal data. N/A

Important — Anthropic data flow: Our backend acts as a proxy. When you submit a request, your browser contacts the LexLens backend server (not Anthropic directly). The LexLens server then forwards the legislative text to the Anthropic Claude API for analysis. Anthropic's API receives the request from the LexLens server, not from your device. Anthropic does not receive your IP address. See Section 7.1 for full details.

IP addresses qualify as personal data under GDPR Article 4(1) because they can potentially be used to identify a natural person. We treat them accordingly.

3.3 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

LexLens does not use cookies, local storage for tracking purposes, or any equivalent tracking technology in Phase 1. The extension operates without persistent client-side identifiers.

Should we introduce cookies or equivalent technologies in a future phase, we will update this policy and implement any required consent mechanisms in compliance with the ePrivacy Directive and applicable national implementing legislation. We will provide advance notice of such changes.

3.4 No Profiling

We do not profile users, build behavioral models, or combine your IP address with any other data to identify you.

We process personal data solely to provide the LexLens service — specifically, to route your analysis request and return a score. We do not use personal data for:

  • Advertising or marketing
  • Building user profiles
  • Selling or sharing with third parties for their own commercial purposes
  • Any purpose unrelated to the immediate analysis request

6. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR / UK GDPR)

Processing ActivityLegal BasisDetails
Processing IP address in transit and in server logs Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) Necessary to route network requests and maintain operational security/debugging capability. No alternative method exists to route requests without IP transmission.
Transmitting legislative text to AI model Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) The text is public legislative content you have chosen to analyze; no personal data is contained within it.

6.1 Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) Summary

We have conducted a Legitimate Interests Assessment for each processing activity above. Key findings:

Purpose test: Processing is for the specific, defined purpose of routing your request and providing the analysis service. There is no secondary use.

Necessity test: IP address transmission is technically unavoidable in TCP/IP networking. Server log retention (30 days) is proportionate and standard industry practice for security incident detection and debugging.

Balancing test: The impact on data subjects is minimal because: (a) IP addresses are not retained beyond 30 days; (b) no profiling or cross-referencing occurs; (c) only publicly available legislative text is analyzed; (d) users retain full control over when they use the service. Our interests in providing a functional, secure service are not overridden by users' privacy interests given these constraints.

Conclusion: Processing is lawful under Article 6(1)(f). We have documented the full LIA internally and will make it available to supervisory authorities on request.

7. Third-Party Data Processors

LexLens uses the following third-party service providers who may process personal data on our behalf:

7.1 Anthropic (Claude API)

  • Role: Sub-processor under GDPR Article 28.
  • Purpose: To generate ideology scores for legislative text submitted by users.
  • Data flow: The LexLens backend server (not the user's browser) contacts the Anthropic API. Anthropic receives the request from the LexLens server.
  • What Anthropic receives: Legislative text submitted for analysis. Anthropic receives the LexLens server's IP address as part of normal network routing — Anthropic does not receive the end user's IP address.
  • What Anthropic does NOT receive: User IP addresses, any user-identifying data, or browsing context.
  • Anthropic's privacy practices: https://www.anthropic.com/privacy
  • DPA status: LexLens has executed a Data Processing Addendum (DPA) with Anthropic under GDPR Article 28. Anthropic's DPA is incorporated by reference into Anthropic's Commercial Terms of Service and is accepted upon account creation. The DPA is available at https://www.anthropic.com/legal/data-processing-addendum.
  • Data retention by Anthropic: For commercial API customers, Anthropic automatically deletes API inputs and outputs within 30 days of receipt or generation. API data is not used to train Anthropic's models. (Note: Anthropic offers a Zero Data Retention (ZDR) arrangement for qualifying enterprise customers, under which inputs and outputs are not retained at all. LexLens will evaluate ZDR as user volume grows.)
  • Transfer safeguards: Anthropic is a U.S.-based company. Data transfers to the U.S. are governed by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — Module Two (controller-to-processor) — incorporated into Anthropic's DPA for EU users, and by the UK ICO Approved Addendum (version B.1.0) for UK users.

We do not share your data with any other third parties.

Legislative text submitted for analysis is processed by Anthropic, a U.S.-based company, via the LexLens backend server.

We ensure such transfers are subject to appropriate safeguards:

  • EU users: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted by the European Commission (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914), Module Two (controller-to-processor), as incorporated into Anthropic's DPA.
  • UK users: The UK ICO Approved Addendum (version B.1.0) to the EU SCCs, as incorporated into Anthropic's DPA.

These safeguards are implemented via Anthropic's Data Processing Addendum, available at https://www.anthropic.com/legal/data-processing-addendum.

Note: End-user IP addresses are not transferred to Anthropic (see Section 7.1). The only data transferred internationally is the legislative text you choose to analyze.

9. Data Retention

LexLens application layer: We do not retain IP addresses or legislative text at the application level beyond the duration of an individual analysis request. Once the request is processed and the result returned, no personal data is stored by the LexLens application.

Server logs: Our backend infrastructure retains standard server access logs — which may include IP addresses, HTTP request metadata, and timestamps — for a maximum of 30 days for security incident detection and debugging purposes. These logs are:

  • Not used for analytics, marketing, profiling, or any commercial purpose
  • Access-restricted to authorized personnel only
  • Deleted automatically after 30 days

This 30-day retention is consistent with the statement in Section 3.2. The reference in Section 3.2 to not storing IP addresses "beyond the duration of the request" refers to application-level storage; server infrastructure logs are retained as described here and in accordance with standard security practices.

16. AI Act Risk Classification

In compliance with the EU AI Act and general transparency principles, LexLens discloses the following:

16.1 AI-Generated Analysis Disclosure

All ideology scores and analytical outputs are generated by an AI system. They are not the opinion of legal or political experts, and they do not constitute legal, political, or electoral advice. Scores are calibrated against academic datasets (Manifesto Project, DW-NOMINATE, Chapel Hill Expert Survey) but carry inherent uncertainty, which is reflected in the confidence scores shown.

16.2 EU AI Act Risk Classification

LexLens has assessed its AI system under the EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) and concludes that it falls within the limited-risk category, not the high-risk category, for the following reasons:

Why LexLens is NOT high-risk (Annex III):

The high-risk AI systems relevant to LexLens's domain are those listed in Annex III, point 8: "AI systems intended to be used to influence the outcome of an election or referendum or the behaviour of natural persons in the casting of their vote in elections or referendums." LexLens does not fall within this category because:

  1. Informational, not influential purpose: LexLens analyzes and presents ideological positioning data derived from academic datasets. It does not recommend how to vote, generate targeted political messaging, or adapt its output based on user identity.
  2. Academic methodology: Scores are computed against established academic benchmarks (CHES, Manifesto Project, DW-NOMINATE) — the same methodology used by political scientists. This is descriptive analysis, not persuasion.
  3. No personalization: Phase 1 has no user accounts. The same legislative text returns the same score for every user. No profiling or micro-targeting is possible.
  4. No action recommendation: LexLens outputs a score and confidence interval. It does not recommend a political course of action, candidate, or party to vote for.
  5. Transparent AI disclosure: All outputs are clearly labeled as AI-generated (Article 50 compliance).

Limited-risk obligations applicable to LexLens:

  • Transparency obligation (Article 50(1)): LexLens discloses that all scores are AI-generated (see Section 16.1 and in-extension UI).

16.3 GPAI Provider Classification

By integrating Claude — a general-purpose AI (GPAI) model developed by Anthropic — into a user-facing product, LexLens likely qualifies as a provider of a GPAI-integrated system under the EU AI Act. Under Article 50(2), providers whose systems generate AI content that users could mistake for human-generated content must ensure that outputs are marked as AI-generated in a machine-readable format and are perceptible to end users.

LexLens complies with this obligation by:

  • Clearly labeling all ideology scores and analytical outputs as AI-generated in the extension UI
  • Including this disclosure in the Privacy Policy (Section 16.1) and in-extension metadata

This Article 50(2) obligation becomes enforceable on 2 August 2026 — within the Phase 1 launch window. LexLens's AI disclosure practices are designed to be compliant from launch.

This classification will be reviewed if LexLens introduces personalization, vote recommendations, or targeted political messaging in future phases.

17.1 Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via:

  • The Chrome Web Store listing update notification
  • A notice within the extension on first use after the update
  • An update to the "Last Updated" date and the version history (Section 20)

Non-material changes (e.g., typographic corrections, clarifications that do not change the substance of our practices) may be made without notice beyond updating this document.

17.2 Phase Transition Plan

LexLens is currently in Phase 1 (no user accounts, anonymous use). Future phases may introduce user accounts, preferences, or expanded data processing. When this occurs:

  • Advance notice: We will publish an updated Privacy Policy at least 30 days before any Phase transition that materially expands data processing.
  • Re-consent: Where required by GDPR or applicable law, we will obtain fresh consent or provide clear opt-in mechanisms before processing any new categories of personal data.
  • User notification: Existing users will be notified via the extension UI at their next use, and via the Chrome Web Store listing.
  • Preserved rights: All rights granted in this policy carry forward into future phases. We will not retroactively change the basis on which data collected in Phase 1 was processed.
